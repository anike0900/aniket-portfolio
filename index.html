// about

A bit about how I got here

I started building small web pages out of curiosity, and that curiosity turned into a genuine passion for crafting things that live on the internet. Today I focus on writing clean, semantic code and designing interfaces that feel obvious to use — even when the problem behind them isn't.

My learning journey has been mostly hands-on: breaking things, rebuilding them, and slowly getting comfortable with the gap between "it works" and "it works well." I care about performance, accessibility, and the small details that make a product feel considered rather than assembled.

Looking ahead, my goal is to keep growing as a frontend engineer, contribute to products that people actually enjoy using, and eventually mentor others who are just starting the same journey I did.
